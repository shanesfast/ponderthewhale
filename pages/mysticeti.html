The Mysticetes of today are all large whales, that filter feed using baleen. They are sometimes referred to as baleen whales, great whales, or whalebone whales. Unlike the Odontocetes, Mysticetes do not have teeth and rely on the keratin-based baleen plates inside their mouths to filter out plankton, krill, and other small prey from the water they take in.

A mother Sperm whale with her calf Image source: Wikimedia The eye of a Right whale Image source: Wikimedia

The keratin found in baleen is a protein that can also be found in mammalian hair, skin, and nails. That includes humans!

A section of baleen Image source: Wikimedia A Gray whale showing off his baleen Image source: Flickr

Mysticetes have some defining characteristics which include:

  • A double blowhole
  • A Symmetrical skull
  • Baleen instead of teeth
  • A sternum consisting of a single bone

Baleen whales behave interestingly. Most of them take long-range seasonal migrations, and many like to launch their entire body out of the water in a brilliant fashion. This jumping out of the water action is known as breaching, which Humpback whales are especially known for. There are currently 4 families of the Mysticeti suborder. They are:

  • Balaenidae
  • Balaenopteridae
  • Eschrichtiidae
  • Neobalaenidae
