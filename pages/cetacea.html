This is the classifcation for all whales, dolphins, and porpoises. They belong to the order Cetacea.


Image source: Wikimedia

Cetacea are mammals. Their group includes some of most distinct and specialized animals on the planet, such as the largest animal in the world: the blue whale.



A Blue Whale Image source: Wikimedia

Cetaceans are solely aquatic mammals. They live their life entirely in water. Most species inhabit coastal areas, as well as the open ocean. Some inhabit freshwater rivers and lakes, while others reside in brackish waters of estuaries and coastal marshes.


An Amazon river dolphin Image source: Wikimedia

Cetacea share a number of physical characteristics. They include:

  • A streamlined body shape (a fusiform)
  • Paddle-shaped front limbs
  • Vestigial hind limbs (inside of their body wall)
  • Laterally flattened tail, bearing horizontal flukes at the tip
  • Thick subcutaneous blubber layer filled with fat and oil
  • Telescoped skull bones
  • External nares on top of the head (blowholes)
  • Internal reproductive organs
  • 3 chambered stomach
  • Basically hairless body (some young have hair on their snouts)

There are two suborders of Cetacea that whales occupy. They are Mysticeti and Odontoceti.
