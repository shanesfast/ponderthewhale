Taxonomy is a widely accepted orginizational system used to classify living things. We use this system to gain a better understanding about different forms of life and how they relate to one another.

The Swedish botanist Carolus Linnaeaus made 2 huge contributions to this system in the mid 1700's when he began publishing his famous pamphlets Systema Naturae. The first contribution was his development of a hierarchical classifcation system. The second was his binomial nomenclature, a 2-part naming system.


Carolus Linnaeus: 1853 portrait by Hendrik Hollander Image source: Wikimedia

You have most likely heard this practice being used at some time in your life. Specific names for living organisms are often referred to as their scientific name. These words, such as "Delphinapterus leucas"; the scientific name for a Beluga whale, are based on Latin and Greek roots and are often hard to pronounce. It's still fun to try.
